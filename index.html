Bio

I am a mathematician mainly interested in the intersection of computer science and statistics. When I was in grad school at Stanford, I spent most of my time studying combinatorics/discrete mathematics and theoretical computer science, thinking about randomized algorithms, optimization problems, and probability theory. I am fortunate to have been advised by Jan Vondrák during my time there. I have also dipped my toes into algebraic topology and algebraic geometry, mainly during my time in undergrad at MIT.

These days, I have shifted my attention to applied disciplines in the finance and tech industries. Shortly after graduating, I started my job at D.E. Shaw as a Quant.
